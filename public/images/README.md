# Public Images Directory

This directory contains the visual assets for the B.S. in Enterprise AI site, curated from the Studio Ordo design system. These assets are ready to be used in UI components.

## Archetype Atlas (`/archetype-atlas/`)
Visuals for different design archetypes and emotional expressions.
- `/faces/archetype-expression.png`: A collage representing a stylistic expression.
- `/walkthroughs/structure-moodboard.png`: Moodboard for structural design themes.
- `/walkthroughs/paradise-moodboard.png`: Moodboard for vibrant, organic themes.
- `/walkthroughs/island-moodboard.png`: Moodboard for isolated, focus-driven themes.
- `/walkthroughs/thumbprint-moodboard.png`: Moodboard for identity-driven, authentic themes.

## Media Modules (`/media/`)
Curated historical graphics and AI-generated editorial imagery illustrating the evolution of the field.
### Generated Editorial Images (`/media/modules/generated/`)
- `era-timeline.svg`: A vector timeline showing the progression of AI eras.
- `era-1-precursors.webp`, `era-2-field-formation.webp`, `era-3-symbolic-programs.webp`, `era-4-expert-systems.webp`, `era-5-statistical-revival.webp`, `era-6-deep-learning.webp`, `era-7-foundation-models.webp`: Thematic illustrations for the 7 major historical eras of AI.
- `era-1-visual-break.webp`, `era-2-visual-break.webp`, etc.: Supplemental visual breaks for era chapters.
- `backpropagation-paper.webp`, `transformer-paper.webp`, `lighthill-report.webp`, `dartmouth-proposal.webp`: Visual representations of landmark research papers and proposals.
- `shannon-theseus-maze.webp`: Image of Claude Shannon’s early mechanical mouse maze.
- `imagenet-moment.webp`: Visual representation of the ImageNet breakthrough.
- `openai-public-ai.webp`: Representation of the current public generative AI phase.
- `representation-learning-bridge.webp`: Conceptual graphic of learning representations.
- `latent-space-landscape-v1.webp`: Abstract landscape of latent space topologies.

## Historical Analogies (`/historical/`)
Visual metaphors illustrating the speed of technological adoption and disruption.
- `easter_1900_no_cars.jpg`: Fifth Avenue on Easter Sunday, 1900. The street is completely dominated by horse-drawn carriages, with only one early automobile visible.
- `easter_1913_no_horses.jpg`: The exact same street on Easter Sunday, 1913. Just 13 years later, the street is completely dominated by automobiles, with only one horse-drawn carriage visible.
